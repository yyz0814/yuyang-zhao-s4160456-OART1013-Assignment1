1.What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

I’ve noticed that all the interactive elements on this website are set against a single background, with content displayed by scrolling up and down. The content appears to float in the air, and switching between different elements is like moving between different layers of clouds.

2.Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

After going to the home page, I selected ‘Omada’, which is a sports-themed scene. I tapped on the football and basketball in the scene; scoring a goal even triggers a coin shower. I also checked the number of coins awarded for each goal and found that the maximum is 99; scoring a goal produces a rather spectacular effect.

3.What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

Interacting with the ball took me the longest.

4.What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

My most common move is to tap the ball and score. I realised there was no top boundary in the basketball game, so I kept tapping the ball to stop it from hitting the ground, only for it to fly off the top of the screen.

5.What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I found the experience very pleasant and quite relaxing. The other interactive elements on the page, with their simple graphics and straightforward interactions, gave me a sense of calm.

6.How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

With its minimalist visual style and playful feedback, this experience feels light-hearted and relaxing. It’s more like a digital stress-relief toy.

7.What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I believe this is an experience characterised by frequent interactions over a short period of time. Thanks to the satisfying gameplay and the accumulation of coins, users can easily squeeze in a couple of quick games during their spare moments.

This experience employs a parallel layout. Initially, floating elements guide me to scroll up and down; when I enter the ‘Omada’ scene and see the striking sphere, the visual focus prompts me to tap. As the interaction deepens, the appearance of gold coins suggests that ‘scoring points’ is the core feedback.
